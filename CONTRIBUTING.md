# Contributing

[中文](CONTRIBUTING.zh-CN.md)

Thank you for improving Family Doctor Skills. Because this project involves health and first-aid guidance, contributions should prioritize safety, clarity, and traceability over completeness.

## Core Principles

- Be conservative in triage.
- Route red flags to real medical care quickly.
- Do not add definitive diagnoses or prescription decisions.
- Use plain language that a stressed non-medical user can follow.
- Add or preserve warnings for high-risk groups.
- Prefer authoritative sources for medical claims.

## What Contributions Are Welcome

- Clearer triage wording
- Better red-flag lists
- Improved child, older adult, pregnancy, allergy, chronic disease, or medication-safety guidance
- Country-specific emergency numbers and care-routing notes
- Safer output templates
- Source updates from authoritative public-health, first-aid, poison-control, or professional medical organizations

## Medical Content Rules

Do not add guidance that:

- Encourages delaying emergency care
- Provides prescription-only medication dosing
- Recommends antibiotics for ordinary viral symptoms
- Advises stopping or changing prescribed medicines without clinician/pharmacist input
- Treats pregnancy, infants, older adults, immune compromise, anticoagulants, or severe allergies as ordinary low-risk cases
- Suggests unsafe home remedies for burns, wounds, poisoning, or fever

If uncertain, choose the safer escalation path.

## Source Expectations

For medical or safety changes, include sources when possible. Good source types include:

- Public-health agencies such as CDC or local equivalents
- First-aid organizations such as American Red Cross or St John Ambulance
- Poison-control organizations
- Professional societies such as ACOG, AAP, AHA, NICE, or equivalent bodies
- Government emergency-care or public-health resources

Avoid using unsourced blog posts, social media, marketing pages, or anecdotal advice as the basis for medical guidance.

## Pull Request Checklist

Before opening a PR, check:

- The change preserves conservative triage.
- Red flags still appear before home-care advice.
- High-risk groups are handled more cautiously.
- Medication advice stays within safe boundaries.
- Wording is clear under stress.
- Sources are included for substantive medical changes.
- Both affected language versions are updated when needed.

## Issue Reports

For safety issues, please include:

- The user prompt or scenario
- The skill response, if available
- Why the advice may be unsafe or incomplete
- Suggested safer wording
- Authoritative source links, if available

Safety-related issues are more important than feature requests.
