# Children

Use for infants, toddlers, school-age children, and teens. Give parent-friendly Chinese guidance. Children can worsen quickly; lower the threshold for pediatric care.

## First Questions

Ask only what is needed:

- Age in months/years and weight if medication questions are involved.
- Main symptom, onset, temperature and measurement method, breathing, feeding/drinking, urine output, mental state, rash, vomiting/diarrhea, pain location, injury mechanism.
- Chronic disease, prematurity, immune problems, medication allergies, current medicines.
- Vaccination status only when relevant; do not turn urgent triage into a vaccine discussion.

## Emergency Now: 120 Or Emergency Department

Escalate immediately for:

- Infant under 3 months with fever or unusually poor feeding/lethargy.
- Trouble breathing, chest retractions, grunting, blue/gray lips, pauses in breathing, or inability to speak/cry because of breathlessness.
- Seizure, stiff neck, severe headache, confusion, hard to wake, floppy child, or persistent inconsolable crying.
- Purple/non-blanching rash, rapidly spreading rash with fever, or swelling of lips/tongue/throat.
- Severe dehydration: no urine for many hours, very dry mouth, no tears, sunken eyes, lethargy, dizziness/fainting.
- Repeated vomiting with inability to keep fluids down, green vomit, blood in vomit/stool, or severe abdominal pain.
- Major trauma, head injury red flags, suspected fracture, deep wound, uncontrolled bleeding, burn involving face/hands/genitals/major joints, or large burn.
- Suspected poisoning, medication ingestion, button battery, magnet ingestion, chemical exposure, or unknown substance ingestion.

## Same-Day Pediatric Care

Recommend same-day pediatric/urgent care for:

- Fever with poor spirit, persistent high fever, fever lasting about 3 days, or fever that returns after improving.
- Ear pain, severe sore throat, trouble swallowing, dehydration risk, worsening cough, wheezing, or suspected pneumonia.
- Vomiting/diarrhea in young children, especially if urine decreases or the child cannot keep fluids down.
- Rash with fever, painful rash, rapidly spreading skin redness, pus, or wound infection signs.
- Pain with urination, severe localized pain, limp/refusal to walk, or persistent abdominal pain.

## Home Care When Mild

Home care is more reasonable when the child is breathing comfortably, alert/interactive, drinking enough to urinate, pain is manageable, and no red flags are present.

Helpful wording:

```
看孩子是否危险，精神状态和呼吸比体温数字更重要。如果孩子能喝水、有尿、能互动、呼吸平稳，通常可以先居家观察；但一旦精神变差、呼吸费力、尿明显减少或抽搐，就要马上就医。
```

## Medication Safety For Children

- Avoid aspirin in children/teens unless a clinician directs it.
- Do not give honey to children under 1 year old.
- Do not recommend adult cold medicines for young children.
- For fever medicine, use the exact product label and weight-based chart; if weight/age/product is unknown, advise pediatrician/pharmacist confirmation.
- Avoid combining multiple cold medicines; many contain overlapping acetaminophen/paracetamol or sedating ingredients.
- Do not use alcohol rubs or heavy covering to "sweat out" fever.

## Parent Output Pattern

```
先看危险信号：{有/没有明显红旗}。

现在最重要看 4 件事：呼吸、精神、喝水、尿量。
1. {处理步骤}
2. {观察点}
3. {复评时间}

如果出现呼吸费力、嘴唇发紫、抽搐、叫不醒/反应差、尿明显减少、反复吐喝不进，或者你觉得孩子明显不对劲，直接去急诊/拨打 120。
```
