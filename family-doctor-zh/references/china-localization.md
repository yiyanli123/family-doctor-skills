# China Localization

Use this file for Chinese-speaking users, especially users in mainland China. Keep answers practical, family-friendly, and conservative.

## Default Chinese Style

- Prefer simplified Chinese unless the user uses another language.
- Start with risk level: "先看是否危险".
- Use concrete routing: "120", "急诊", "当天门诊/儿科", "社区卫生服务中心", "居家观察".
- Avoid frightening language unless the situation is urgent. Be direct for emergencies.
- Give short step lists. Most users asking during illness are stressed and need action, not a lecture.

## Mainland China Care Routing

Emergency now: advise calling 120 for life-threatening symptoms, severe trauma, major bleeding, severe breathing difficulty, suspected stroke/heart attack, severe allergic reaction, unconsciousness, seizure, severe poisoning, or rapidly worsening condition.

Hospital emergency department: recommend urgent ED evaluation for serious but transportable conditions such as severe/worsening abdominal pain, significant dehydration, high fever with concerning symptoms, suspected fracture, deep wounds, head injury red flags, or symptoms in high-risk groups.

Same-day outpatient/children's clinic/community care: use for stable but concerning symptoms, persistent fever, suspected bacterial infection needing testing, wound checks, worsening cough, ear pain, urinary symptoms, moderate dehydration risk, or medication questions.

Home observation: use only for mild, stable, improving symptoms with no red flags, adequate hydration/breathing, manageable pain, and ability to seek care if worse.

## Calling 120

Tell the user or bystander to:

1. Say the exact location first, including city, district, street, building, gate, floor, room, and landmarks.
2. Describe the main problem, age, sex, consciousness, breathing, bleeding, chest pain, stroke signs, injury mechanism, or suspected poisoning.
3. Leave a reachable phone number and keep the phone open.
4. Send someone to guide the ambulance if possible.
5. Do not move the person after major trauma, suspected spine injury, stroke-like symptoms, severe chest pain, or altered consciousness unless there is immediate environmental danger.
6. Prepare ID/医保卡 if available, current medicines, allergy history, medical records, test results, and substance containers/medicine boxes for poisoning or medication errors.

## High-Risk Groups

Be more cautious for the groups below. For details, load `children.md`, `older-adults.md`, or `pregnancy-allergy-high-risk.md`.

- Infants under 3 months, especially any fever or poor feeding.
- Children with breathing difficulty, lethargy, dehydration, convulsions, persistent high fever, or rash with fever.
- Pregnant or postpartum users.
- Older adults, especially frailty, confusion, falls, chest symptoms, or dehydration.
- People with heart/lung/kidney/liver disease, diabetes, cancer treatment, transplant, HIV with immune compromise, high-dose steroids, or anticoagulant use.

Chinese reminder:

```
这个人群本身风险更高，所以分流要更保守：同样的发热、腹泻、外伤或过敏，在普通成年人可以观察，但在婴幼儿、老人、孕产妇、免疫低下或慢病患者身上，常常需要更早联系医生或当天就医。
```

## Common China-Specific Pitfalls

- Do not recommend antibiotics for ordinary colds or viral flu-like illness. Antibiotics require clinician judgment and are not fever reducers.
- Do not recommend routine IV drips/infusions for common colds, mild fever, or mild dehydration when oral fluids are tolerated.
- Do not recommend combining multiple cold medicines. Many contain overlapping fever reducers or sedating ingredients.
- Do not recommend alcohol rubbing for fever, especially in children.
- Do not recommend covering a feverish child heavily to "sweat it out".
- Do not recommend toothpaste, soy sauce, sesame oil, herbal powders, or other home remedies on burns or wounds.
- Do not recommend delaying care for stroke-like symptoms to "observe for a while".
- Do not recommend driving oneself to hospital with chest pain, fainting, severe dizziness, severe allergic reaction, or stroke-like symptoms.

## Chinese Emergency Phrases

Use these as needed:

```
这属于危险信号，不建议继续在家观察。请现在拨打 120。等待救护车时，让患者平躺或保持最舒服的呼吸姿势，不要吃喝；如果是外伤出血，持续直接按压止血；如果是疑似中毒，带上药瓶/包装/剩余物。
```

```
目前更像是需要当天就医评估，而不是单纯在家扛过去。建议今天去急诊/门诊，尤其是如果症状在加重、喝不进水、尿明显减少、精神变差，或出现呼吸困难。
```

```
如果没有这些危险信号，可以先居家处理和观察。但要设一个复评点：如果 {时间} 后没有改善，或者出现 {红旗}，就升级为当天就医。
```

## Visit Preparation

For clinic or ED visits, suggest bringing:

- 身份证/医保卡 if relevant.
- Current medicines, supplements, and allergy history.
- Temperature log, symptom timeline, photos of rash/wound/stool/vomit if appropriate.
- Existing diagnoses and recent test reports.
- For poisoning/medication error: original package, bottle, label, estimated amount, and time of exposure.
