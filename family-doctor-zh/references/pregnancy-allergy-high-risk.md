# Pregnancy, Allergy, Chronic Disease, And Immune Risk

Use for pregnancy/postpartum, allergy-prone users, asthma, chronic disease, immune compromise, anticoagulants, recent surgery, and complex medication situations. Be conservative.

## Pregnancy And Postpartum

Ask gestational week or postpartum days/weeks, main symptom, bleeding/fluid leakage, abdominal pain/contractions, fetal movement if applicable, blood pressure if known, fever, headache/vision symptoms, swelling, chest/breathing symptoms, and obstetric history.

Emergency or urgent obstetric care for:

- Vaginal bleeding more than spotting, bleeding with abdominal pain, or any heavy bleeding.
- Severe abdominal pain, persistent contractions before term, fluid leakage, or suspected labor concerns.
- Decreased/absent fetal movement after the user normally tracks fetal movement.
- Severe headache, vision changes, severe swelling, right upper abdominal pain, very high blood pressure if known, seizure, fainting, or confusion.
- Fever with feeling very unwell, shortness of breath, chest pain, calf swelling/pain, severe vomiting/dehydration.
- Postpartum heavy bleeding, fever, severe pelvic/abdominal pain, chest pain, shortness of breath, severe headache/vision changes, or thoughts of self-harm.

Medication rule: avoid recommending medicines in pregnancy/breastfeeding unless clearly OTC-label safe and low risk; advise obstetrician/pharmacist confirmation.

## Allergy-Prone Users And Anaphylaxis

Ask trigger, timing, symptoms, prior anaphylaxis, asthma, epinephrine auto-injector availability, and current breathing/swallowing status.

Emergency now for:

- Trouble breathing, wheezing, throat tightness, hoarse voice, lip/tongue/throat swelling, trouble swallowing saliva.
- Hives or swelling with dizziness, fainting, vomiting/diarrhea, chest tightness, or feeling of impending doom.
- Rapidly progressing symptoms after food, medicine, insect sting, vaccine, latex, or unknown trigger.

Action:

- Call 120/local emergency services.
- If the user has a prescribed epinephrine auto-injector, advise using it according to its instructions and still seeking emergency care.
- Keep the person lying down if dizzy/faint, or seated upright if breathing is easier that way. Do not give food or drink if throat/breathing symptoms are present.
- Do not rely on antihistamines alone for breathing/throat/circulation symptoms.

## Asthma, COPD, Heart Disease, Diabetes, Kidney/Liver Disease

Lower the threshold for same-day care when symptoms involve fever, dehydration, breathing, chest pain, weakness, vomiting/diarrhea, medication side effects, or abnormal home measurements.

Escalate urgently for:

- Asthma/COPD: worsening shortness of breath, trouble speaking, blue lips, low oxygen, rescue inhaler not helping as usual, drowsiness/confusion.
- Heart disease: chest pressure, shortness of breath at rest, fainting, new severe swelling, rapid/irregular heartbeat with symptoms.
- Diabetes: confusion, severe weakness, vomiting, dehydration, very high or very low glucose, ketone concern, infection with worsening glucose control.
- Kidney/liver disease: dehydration, confusion, bleeding, severe swelling, reduced urine, medication toxicity concern.

## Immune Compromise

Includes chemotherapy, transplant medicines, high-dose steroids, biologics, advanced HIV, severe immune disorders, or clinician-described immune suppression.

- Fever, chills, new cough, shortness of breath, wound redness, urinary symptoms, diarrhea, or feeling acutely unwell should generally prompt clinician contact the same day.
- Do not reassure solely because symptoms seem mild.
- Avoid advising OTC fever reducers as a way to delay evaluation when fever may signal serious infection.

## Anticoagulants And Bleeding Risk

Includes warfarin, rivaroxaban, apixaban, dabigatran, edoxaban, heparin, and sometimes dual antiplatelet therapy.

Urgent evaluation for:

- Head injury, significant fall, unusual severe headache, weakness/numbness, confusion.
- Black stool, blood in stool/urine/vomit, coughing blood, uncontrolled nosebleed, large unexplained bruises, or prolonged bleeding.
- Severe abdominal/back pain or fainting.

Avoid recommending NSAIDs unless clinician-approved because bleeding/kidney risks may be higher.

## Complex Medication Use

When the user takes many medicines or has unclear drug names:

- Ask for exact medicine names, doses, timing, and recent changes.
- Encourage pharmacist/clinician review before adding OTC cold, sleep, pain, or allergy medicines.
- Watch duplicate acetaminophen/paracetamol, sedating antihistamines, NSAIDs, decongestants, and interactions with anticoagulants, blood pressure medicines, diabetes medicines, antidepressants, and sedatives.
