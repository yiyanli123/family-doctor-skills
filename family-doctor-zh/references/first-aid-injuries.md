# First Aid For Common Injuries

Use for cuts, bleeding, burns, sprains/strains, head injury, bites, splinters, and choking.

For Chinese users, give direct steps first, then explain. Use "按压止血", "流动凉水冲洗", "不要抹牙膏/酱油/偏方", and "必要时 120/急诊".

## Bleeding And Cuts

Immediate care:

- Wash hands if possible.
- Apply firm direct pressure with clean gauze/cloth.
- Keep pressure continuous for several minutes; add layers if blood soaks through.
- Once bleeding is controlled, rinse minor wounds with clean running water, remove visible dirt if easy, apply a clean dressing, and monitor.

Escalate:

- Call emergency services for uncontrolled, spurting, or life-threatening bleeding.
- Seek same-day care for deep/gaping wounds, face/hand/genital/joint wounds, dirty wounds, bites, retained foreign body, numbness/weakness, or possible tetanus need.

## Burns

Immediate care:

- Cool thermal burns under cool running water for about 20 minutes if practical.
- Remove jewelry/tight items near the burn early if not stuck.
- Cover with a clean nonstick dressing or clean cloth.

Avoid:

- Do not apply ice directly.
- Do not pop blisters.
- Do not apply butter, toothpaste, or home remedies.
- Do not apply soy sauce, sesame oil, herbal powders, alcohol, or unknown ointments to fresh burns.

Escalate:

- Emergency care for large, deep, chemical/electrical, inhalation, face/hand/genital/major-joint burns, or burns with breathing symptoms.

## Sprains And Strains

Home care if mild and no deformity/neurovascular symptoms:

- Rest, protect the area, use cold packs wrapped in cloth for short intervals, elevate, and consider compression if comfortable.
- Use OTC pain relief only if safe and label-consistent.

Seek care for:

- Deformity, severe pain, inability to bear weight/use limb, numbness, weakness, cold/pale limb, open wound, or worsening swelling/pain.

## Head Injury

Emergency care for:

- Loss of consciousness, seizure, repeated vomiting, worsening headache, confusion, unusual behavior, weakness/numbness, unequal pupils, blood/clear fluid from nose/ears, anticoagulant use with significant head impact, or high-energy mechanism.

For minor bumps without red flags:

- Observe closely for 24 hours, rest, avoid alcohol/sedatives, and seek care if symptoms worsen.

## Choking

If the person cannot cough, speak, cry, or breathe, call emergency services and use age-appropriate choking first aid. For infants, use infant-specific back blows/chest thrusts; for older children/adults, use standard choking maneuvers. If the person becomes unresponsive, start CPR if trained and follow dispatcher instructions.
