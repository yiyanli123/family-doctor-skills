# Gastrointestinal Illness And Poisoning

Use for vomiting, diarrhea, stomach pain, foodborne illness concerns, dehydration, medication mistakes, chemical exposures, and suspected poisoning.

For Chinese users, route severe poisoning to 120/急诊 and advise bringing the original bottle/package/label. If a local poison-control number is known to the user, they can call it; otherwise use emergency services.

## Vomiting Or Diarrhea

Triage:

- Age, duration, number of episodes, blood/black stool, fever, severe abdominal pain, pregnancy, immune compromise.
- Hydration: urine frequency, dizziness, dry mouth, tears in children, ability to keep fluids down.
- Exposure: questionable food, sick contacts, travel, antibiotics, new medicine.

Home care if mild and no red flags:

- Use small frequent sips of oral rehydration solution or fluids.
- Resume bland/easy foods as tolerated; avoid forcing food.
- Avoid alcohol and very sugary drinks if diarrhea is significant.
- Monitor urine output and energy.

Seek urgent care for:

- Blood in stool/vomit, severe or localized abdominal pain, rigid abdomen, persistent high fever, signs of dehydration, inability to keep fluids down, symptoms in infants/older frail adults/pregnancy/immunocompromise, or worsening symptoms.

## Poisoning Or Medication Error

Ask quickly:

- What substance/medicine, amount, time, route, age/weight, symptoms, and container/label availability.

Routing:

- US: call Poison Control at 1-800-222-1222 or use poison.org for immediate expert guidance.
- Mainland China: call 120 or go to emergency care for significant ingestion/exposure, uncertain dose, child ingestion, intentional overdose, chemical exposure, or any symptoms. Bring the package/container.
- Call emergency services now if the person has trouble breathing, seizure, unconsciousness, severe sleepiness, chest pain, severe agitation, or other serious symptoms.

Avoid:

- Do not induce vomiting unless Poison Control or emergency professionals explicitly instruct it.
- Do not give food, drink, or activated charcoal unless instructed by professionals.
- Do not wait for symptoms after potentially dangerous ingestion; route to Poison Control.

## Abdominal Pain

Escalate same day or emergency depending on severity for:

- Severe, worsening, or localized pain; right lower quadrant pain; pain with pregnancy; chest pain; fainting; black/bloody stools; vomiting blood; swollen/rigid abdomen; or fever with significant pain.
