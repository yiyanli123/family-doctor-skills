# English Localization

Use this file for English-speaking users. Keep answers practical, direct, and conservative.

## Emergency Numbers And Care Paths

- US/Canada: 911 for life-threatening emergencies.
- UK/Ireland: 999 or 112 for emergencies; NHS 111 may be appropriate for urgent non-emergency advice in the UK.
- Australia: 000 for emergencies.
- US poisoning questions: Poison Control at 1-800-222-1222 or poison.org; call 911 for severe symptoms.
- If the country is unknown, say "call your local emergency number."

Care routing:

- Emergency services: life-threatening symptoms or unsafe self-transport.
- ER/ED: severe or rapidly worsening symptoms needing hospital evaluation.
- Urgent care or same-day clinician contact: stable but concerning symptoms.
- Primary care/GP: non-emergency symptoms that persist, recur, or need diagnosis.
- Pharmacist: OTC medicine questions when symptoms are mild and no red flags exist.
- Home care: mild, stable, no red flags, and able to seek care if worse.

## Calling Emergency Services

Tell the user or bystander to:

1. Give the exact location first.
2. Describe the main problem, age, consciousness, breathing, bleeding, chest pain, stroke signs, injury mechanism, or suspected poisoning.
3. Keep the phone available and follow dispatcher instructions.
4. Send someone to meet emergency responders if safe.
5. Do not move the person after major trauma, suspected spine injury, stroke-like symptoms, severe chest pain, or altered consciousness unless there is immediate environmental danger.
6. Prepare medicine bottles, allergy history, medical conditions, and substance containers if poisoning or medication error is involved.

## Common Pitfalls

- Do not recommend antibiotics for ordinary colds or viral flu-like illness.
- Do not recommend delaying care for stroke-like symptoms to "wait and see."
- Do not recommend driving oneself with chest pain, fainting, severe dizziness, severe allergic reaction, or stroke-like symptoms.
- Do not recommend ice directly on burns, popping blisters, butter, toothpaste, oils, or home remedies on burns or wounds.
- Do not recommend combining multiple cold medicines without checking active ingredients.

## Emergency Wording

```
This is not a good situation to watch at home. Please call emergency services now. If you are in the US or Canada, call 911; in the UK or Ireland, call 999 or 112; in Australia, call 000. While waiting, {position/pressure/no food or drink/bring medication bottle}.
```

## Visit Preparation

For clinic, urgent care, or ER visits, suggest bringing:

- ID/insurance information if relevant.
- Current medicines, supplements, and allergy history.
- Symptom timeline, temperature log, photos of rash/wound/stool/vomit if appropriate.
- Existing diagnoses and recent test reports.
- For poisoning/medication error: original package, bottle, label, estimated amount, and time of exposure.
