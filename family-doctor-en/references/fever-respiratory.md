# Fever And Respiratory Symptoms

Use for fever, common cold, cough, sore throat, flu-like illness, COVID-like illness, sinus symptoms, and mild respiratory infections.

## Triage Questions

Ask as needed:

- Age, pregnancy status, immune compromise, major chronic disease.
- Temperature value, measurement method, duration, and response to fluids/rest/OTC medicine.
- Breathing symptoms: shortness of breath, wheezing, chest pain, blue lips, oxygen saturation if available.
- Hydration: urine frequency, ability to drink, vomiting/diarrhea.
- Associated symptoms: stiff neck, rash, confusion, severe headache, ear pain, sore throat severity, cough duration, sputum/blood.
- Exposure: flu/COVID/strep contacts, travel, vaccination status if relevant.

## Home Care

For mild symptoms without red flags:

- Rest and drink fluids. Use small frequent sips if nauseated.
- Use a humidifier, saline spray, honey for cough in people over 1 year old, and warm fluids for throat comfort.
- Consider OTC fever/pain medicine only if safe for the person and consistent with the product label. Avoid aspirin in children/teens unless directed by a clinician.
- Reduce spread with hand hygiene, ventilation, masking around vulnerable people, and staying home while acutely ill when possible.
- Reassess if symptoms worsen, fever persists, breathing changes, or new red flags appear.

For fever in children, emphasize comfort, hydration, breathing, mental status, and urine output rather than temperature alone.

## Seek Care

Recommend urgent evaluation for:

- Any emergency or same-day red flag from `red-flags.md`.
- Fever in infants under 3 months.
- Fever lasting more than about 3 days, recurring fever, or fever with severe localized pain.
- Flu-like illness in high-risk people where antiviral timing may matter.
- Sore throat with trouble swallowing saliva, muffled voice, neck swelling, dehydration, rash, or high suspicion for strep needing testing.
- Cough lasting more than 3 weeks, coughing blood, chest pain, or worsening after initial improvement.

## Avoid

- Do not recommend antibiotics for ordinary colds or viral symptoms.
- Do not recommend cough/cold medicines for young children unless label and clinician guidance support it.
- Do not give honey to children under 1 year old.
- Do not stack multiple OTC products containing acetaminophen/paracetamol.
