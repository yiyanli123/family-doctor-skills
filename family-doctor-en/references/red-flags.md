# Red Flags And Escalation

Use this before topic-specific advice. If any emergency red flag is present, advise immediate emergency services first.

## Emergency Now

Call local emergency services now for:

- Severe trouble breathing, gasping, choking that is not relieved, or blue/gray lips or face.
- Chest pain/pressure, fainting with chest symptoms, or suspected heart attack.
- Stroke signs: face droop, arm weakness, speech trouble, sudden confusion, sudden severe headache, sudden vision loss, or sudden trouble walking.
- Severe allergic reaction: trouble breathing, throat/tongue/lip swelling, widespread hives with dizziness/vomiting, or known anaphylaxis.
- Uncontrolled or spurting bleeding, bleeding soaking through dressings, deep wound with exposed tissue, or amputation.
- Major trauma, suspected neck/spine injury, severe head injury, repeated vomiting after head injury, seizure after injury, or loss of consciousness.
- New seizure, seizure lasting more than 5 minutes, repeated seizures, or seizure with injury/pregnancy/diabetes.
- Altered mental status, severe drowsiness, hard to wake, severe weakness, or signs of shock.
- Severe burns, burns involving face/hands/genitals/major joints, electrical/chemical burns, inhalation injury, or large blistering burns.
- Poisoning/overdose with severe symptoms: unconsciousness, seizure, severe sleepiness, trouble breathing, chest pain, repeated vomiting, or dangerous substance ingestion.
- Suicidal intent, violent behavior, or immediate risk of self-harm or harm to others.

## Same-Day Urgent Care Or Clinician Contact

Recommend same-day care for:

- Fever in an infant under 3 months, or any baby with poor feeding, lethargy, breathing trouble, or dehydration.
- Fever with stiff neck, confusion, purple/non-blanching rash, severe headache, persistent vomiting, severe abdominal pain, or immune compromise.
- Shortness of breath, wheezing not improving with the usual plan, oxygen saturation below the user's known safe range, or worsening respiratory symptoms.
- Dehydration: very little urine, dizziness/fainting, dry mouth with inability to keep fluids down, sunken eyes in children, or no tears.
- A wound that is deep, gaping, dirty, caused by an animal/human bite, has a foreign body, crosses a joint/face/hand/genitals, or may need a tetanus update.
- Redness, warmth, swelling, pus, red streaks, fever, or worsening pain around a wound.
- Severe pain, deformity, inability to bear weight/use limb, numbness, weakness, or suspected fracture.
- Symptoms in pregnancy, significant heart/lung/kidney/liver disease, cancer therapy, transplant, high-dose steroids, or other immune compromise.

## Home Care Is More Reasonable When

Home monitoring may be appropriate when symptoms are mild, stable or improving, there are no red flags, hydration and breathing are adequate, pain is manageable, and the user can access care if symptoms worsen.

## Regional Routing

If location is unknown, say "call your local emergency number." In the US and Canada, use 911. In the UK/Ireland, use 999 or 112. In Australia, use 000. For US poison questions, use Poison Control at 1-800-222-1222 or poison.org unless severe symptoms require 911.
